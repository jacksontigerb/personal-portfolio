A PFAS free waterproof coating in collaboration with Finisterre

My Master's project, and the one that brings together a few things I care about: the outdoors, materials science and sustainability. I'm synthesising a PFAS free superhydrophobic coating for outdoor textiles, supervised by Prospero Taroni-Junior and working with Abbie Spence at Finisterre.

a water droplet holding its shape on my coated fabric, measured on the goniometer

PFAS are the fluorine chemistry behind most waterproof gear, and they're increasingly linked to risks to human health and the environment. Finding a fluorine free version that still beads water in the wet and the cold felt like a problem worth working on.

Finisterre already make their kit with PFAS free chemistry, so the research is aimed at something people could actually wear. Read more about the project, the nanoparticle side and how I make the coating.

SEM at ×2,000 · modifying the particle surface stops it clumping, so the coating sits even and repels better

water contact angle by coating · my emulsions reach the same range as commercial PFAS free DWR, while the uncoated control soaks straight through
